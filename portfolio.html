Private Fly-In Living

Fly In.
Live Large.

Pegasus Airpark isn't a neighborhood that happens to be near an airport. It's a community built around the runway — where you taxi from your hangar, clear the gate, and you're airborne. No commute. No FBO. Just home.

Building in Pegasus requires a builder who understands the client. Hangar integration, taxiway clearances, and the exacting standards of someone who measures tolerance in feet above ground — this is not a project for a standard production builder.

Matthew Juris holds both a contractor's license and 17 years of luxury real estate experience in the Arizona market. He knows what a Pegasus home needs to be — and he builds it that way.

What Sets This
Build Apart

Most builders meet code. We exceed it — deliberately, at every layer. Here's what that looks like in practice on a Prestige Build Co. home.

Envelope & Structure

Most builders use the cheapest materials that still pass inspection. We spec what actually protects the home — and your energy bills — for decades. In Arizona's climate, the envelope is everything.

▸ Tyvek® HomeWrap® — not standard #15 lb tar paper
▸ Westlake Tiles Seal HT Synthetic Underlayment — not asphalt saturated felt
▸ 10" closed-cell spray foam at roof deck — R38
▸ 5" spray foam at all exterior walls including garage — R19
▸ 6" spray foam at garage roof deck — R23
▸ 5" concrete slab with fiber mesh and wire mesh reinforcement

Main House Kitchen

Panel-ready appliances throughout means seamless cabinetry integration — no visible branding, no visual interruption. The kitchen is built around how serious cooks and discerning buyers actually use a space.

▸ 48" dual fuel range
▸ Dual panel-ready Cove dishwashers
▸ Panel-ready 36" Sub-Zero refrigerator & 36" Sub-Zero range
▸ Wolf 48" pro hood & Wolf micro drawer
▸ Hidden walk-in pantry with panel-ready Scotsman ice maker
▸ Panel-ready Monogram 30" wine fridge with lower freezer drawer

Details That Matter

The casita is fully self-contained — not an afterthought. The fireplaces are masonry, not prefab. These are the decisions that separate a home built to impress from one built to sell quickly.
